MEET DESIGNER VICTORIA T. MÜLLER

Victoria understood at age 24 what the fine jewelry market was missing: Approachable-priced, thoughtfully designed pieces made for women like herself. Upon recognizing this void, Victoria began to design and create her own pieces with her sister from their family-run fine jewelry business, Müller Jewelry Collection, in Belgium - in the late 1990 and made precious gold pieces for private clients.

In 2012, Victoria began selling her pieces local hair salon in the USA, where she continued selling for the next 3 years. During this time, Victoria steadily built up Direct to Consumer Clientele, began utilizing her role in Charity Driven platform, and made the entree into wholesale, with Shopbop.com. By 2014, MJC secured it's first luxury department store partner - Bergdorf Goodman, forever changing the landscape of and introducing fine jewelry on the 5th Floor (5F).

Today, Victoria continues to create pieces using 14K gold handset with diamonds, precious stones, and enamel. Undertstated and simple, yet glamorous MJC offers a beautiful balance between relevant trends and timeless design. Pushing boundaries and expanding the offering, Victoria lunched her Bridal Collection in Spring 2019, following the death of her Co-partner, Will Strauss, who died in 2019 from Lung Cancer. All while staying true to the brand's DNA of approachable and affordable fine jewelry.
